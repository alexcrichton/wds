Expected weather
In the past 6 years, the average temperature was 81 F in the afternoon and 76 F in the evening. The average humidity was 46% in the afternoon and 65% in the evening. There was light rain in the evening on this day three years out of the past six years (50% probability). We recommend dressing for warm weather for the afternoon, and possibly bringing a light jacket and umbrella for the evening.
General
The bride prefers that you wear semi-formal clothing, with the understanding that you may not want to purchase new clothing to wear only once for this event. The bride will be happy shop for, and even purchase or rent, affordable clothing options that both suit your style and satisfy the dress code for the event. That said, the minimum level of formality for clothing is business casual, please see below for details. If you have any questions or would like help with shopping/purchasing clothing, please contact the bride. Feel free to send her photos of your potential outfit and she will be happy to advise you!
Women's dress code
Business Casual: blouse/button-up shirt with slacks/khakis/skirt
Semi-Formal: dress, ao dai (Vietnamese traditional dress), pant/skirt suit
Skirt or dress length can be either long or short.
Color: If possible, the bride prefers that ladies wear light/pastel colors, like this. In order not to mirror the colors of the wedding dress and the wedding party, we ask that ladies avoid large blocks of white, ivory, and silver. The bride also prefers that ladies additionally avoid large blocks of dark colors and red (like this and this).
Men's dress code
The bride prefers that men wear suits, or a sports jacket with dress slacks. A long sleeved button up shirt and long pants are required. Please do not wear polos, short sleeves, or T-shirts. Khakis are fine but not jeans or shorts. Ties are not required but certainly welcome. Color: In order not to mirror the color of the wedding party and the groom, we ask that men avoid light gray/silver and black.
